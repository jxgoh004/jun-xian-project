Inside bar spring — a brief break-below after a tight pullback, recovered by the next bar.

How the strategy works

The inside bar spring is a bullish reversal pattern. After a wide mother bar, price coils into a narrow inside bar, then briefly breaks below the mother low (the spring) and is recovered by the next bar — the confirmation. We simulate buying at the open of the bar after confirmation, with stop at the lowest low of the 5-bar window and target at twice the risk (1:2 R-multiple).

Entry
Open of the bar after confirmation
Stop
Min(low) across the 5-bar pattern window
Target
Entry + 2 × (entry − stop)
Timeout
None — held until stop or target hits
Intrabar
Pessimistic — stop wins ties when both hit in one bar
Confirmation types
Pin, Mark-up, Ice cream — three bullish recovery shapes
